About My Engine

Overview

This engine started out as a tutorial project following The Cherno's game engine series. I made it through about five or six videos or so before realizing I want to just make this project my own way. No fault to him, it is a great series; I just wanted something built that I could call mine, even if it turned out terrible. This engine does not really have a name, all I know is I just want it to have the capability of making a variety of voxel games. Apart from the obvious areas like the core, main application setup, Layers (which I am trying to figure out how to implement into what I have going on now), and the logger, everything is 100% mine. I am not using this about page to brag about my engine in any way, if anyone reads this use it as inspiration more than anything. If I can make a game engine, I am 1,000,000% certain you can as well!

Inspiration

I have always been interested in programming, more specifically game development. The issue is I am about as far away from an artist as one can get. I consistently used this as a crutch, never learned art, and pretty much blamed art skills on my lack of producing games. So, I jumped into the next best thing, making an engine for games. May not be the best decision ever, but I am having way more of a blast doing this than blaming my artistic endeavors. On top of this, I will 100% feel much more accomplished making a couple very tiny games built on my own engine with terrible art than making games with an existing engine.

Throughout this journey I been reading Game Engine Archetecture Volume 3, Real Time Rendering, and I have a Vulcan book I have not even opened yet. So, I feel like these resources deserve a bit of a shoutout. Regardless, in the future I would definitely enjoy getting into game development with means of outsourcing everything besides programming and level design. My current situations do not allow me to do this. Maybe sometime though.

Future Endeavors

I plan on just going with the flow to be honest. I know for sure I am not going to add multiplayer capabilities or ray tracing. However, my end goal is to have a voxel engine with a usable editor, scripting, and capable of making a game from start to finish. I will add things like mesh importers and everything else. As it currently stands (2/22/2026) I still have tons to worry about in the root systems than to add these exciting features. But, that is the plan at least!
